Rewards structure

The card_name offers four different tiers of rewards consumers can maximize. After signing up, cardholders earn cash back at the following rates:

  • 4% back on up to $7,000 per year in gas purchases worldwide and EV charging stations (then 1% back)
  • 3% back on restaurant purchases and eligible travel
  • 2% back on Costco and Costco.com purchases
  • 1% back on everything else

With this rewards structure in mind, it's easy to see how you could rack up a ton of cash back on gas purchases, groceries at Costco stores, dining, travel and more. However, you should know about the very unique way the card_name doles out rewards before you sign up for this card.

Instead of letting you rack up cash back you can use throughout the year whenever you're ready, this card only gives you your cash back in the form of a rewards check once per year when your February statement closes. This means you can't save up your cash back to use at your leisure, and that you'll have to wait 12 months to be able to access your rewards haul each year.

Not only that, but rewards are only redeemable in Costco stores for Costco merchandise. You may be able to use your Costco rewards check to get cash back at a Costco register too, but the fine print on the card_name says individual Costco stores can allow this (or not allow it) based on their own discretion.

What could be improved?

While the card_name has an excellent rewards rate by any measure, there are a few notable downsides to be aware of. Improvements that could dramatically improve the value of this card include the following:

  • The card doesn't have a sign-up bonus. Other cash back credit cards offer generous rewards plus a cash bonus of $200 or more, yet the card_name doesn't have a welcome offer of any kind.
  • The card_name lacks an introductory APR offer. This card comes with a high interest rate that applies when you carry a balance, and the same APR applies to balance transfers.
  • The earning rate for Costco purchases and other spending could be higher. Earning 2% back at Costco is just okay since other cards offer 2% cash back on everything you buy, and the 1% rewards rate on other purchases is seriously lacking.

Frequently Asked Questions (FAQ)

Is Costco changing credit cards again?

Costco credit cards used to be issued by American Express, but the two companies ended their partnership in 2016. As of today, Costco credit cards for personal and business use are issued by Citi instead.

What is a good credit score to get a Costco credit card?

The Costco credit card requires good or excellent credit, so having a credit score of 670 or higher gives you the best chance at approval.

How do I get my Costco 4% back?

The Costco Anywhere Visa® Card by Citi lets you earn 4% back on the first $7,000 you spend on gas worldwide and EV charging each year, then 1% back.
