Time is arguably the most precious thing that a person can have. However, the device which tell time may be almost as valuable as time itself. How much are you willing to give for the most expensive watch on our planet?

It is not a secret that watches are considered to be among the most expensive jewellery items. Thus, what is the maximum price limit for them? What is the most expensive watch in the world?

The most expensive watch: compilation of top 10 unique pieces of jewellery

Everything has its price. In the case of watches, this price is usually impressive. However, the cost is justified, without a doubt. The efforts put into the creation of one such item are immense. Sometimes, it takes years to craft one. Add precious materials that were meticulously selected for the jewellery, and you will get a price tag that has a figure with many zeros written on it. So, how much money do you need to get the most costly watches available on the market?

1. Graff Diamonds Hallucination, $55 million

This piece of art is truly the most expensive watch in the world with a stunning figure of $55 million written on its price tag. Ever since its creation in 2014 by Graff Luxury House, Hallucination proudly keeps its title of the world’s most expensive watch. This fascinating jewellery item is obviously designed for a woman with its wide platinum bracelet coated in 110 carats of multicoloured diamonds.

The most expensive watch ever created gets its cool price not from the intricate and precise mechanisms spinning the hands but from the rarest stones that adorn the jewellery piece. You can see diamonds of blue, pink, yellow, olive, and amber colours on this luxurious accessory. Laurence Graff, the head of the luxury house, is immensely proud about the result of many years of hard work. As he said in one of his interviews:

2. Graff Diamonds The Fascination, $40 million

This second most expensive watch in the world was also designed and created by the geniuses of the Graff Luxury House. The Fascination is a truly unique accessory. It is a convertible piece of jewellery. The central drop-shaped part of the item can be removed and worn as a separate ring. The bracelet of the watch can also be worn without its dial. Thus, for $40 million you get not only a watch but also a fancy ring with a bracelet.

3. Breguet Grande Complication Marie-Antoinette, $30 million

Marie-Antoinette is not the most expensive watch but is certainly among the ones with the longest history. The name of the jewellery item is not someone’s whim. The piece was crafted specially for the royal woman. Unfortunately, the queen died about 30 years before the item was completed. The historical accessory was ready only in 1827. Today, you can find the original piece of art in the Mayer Museum, Los Angeles, USA.

4. Jaeger-LeCoultre Joaillerie 101 Manchette, $26 million

This jewellery item is a true piece of art. At first glance, it appears to be a beautiful geometrical bracelet sprinkled with sparkling diamonds. However, if you look closely, you may spot a tiny dial among its square sections and 576 diamonds. Due to this miniature dial, the Joaillerie 101 Manchette contains the smallest mechanical movement in the world. This is the feature that makes this luxury piece so unique.

5. Chopard 201 Carat, $25 million

If you lack $55 million for the Graff Diamonds Hallucination but want a similar item, Chopard 201 Carat for $25 million may be just for you. The luxurious accessory contains 874 diamonds of various shades. The central part of the bracelet has three big diamonds that open up and reveal a dial when a special mechanism is pressed.

6. Patek Philippe Henry Graves Supercomplication, $24 million

“Supercomplication” part of the name of the accessory really reflects the intricacies of the item. It has over 24 features and, due to this, remains the most complicated handmade wristwatch in the world. It can display times of sunrises and sunsets, a calendar, and a celestial map, among other things. This piece of jewellery may not be the most expensive watch ever sold, but it is certainly the best example of mechanical art.

7. Jacob & Co. Billionaire Watch, $18 million

This luxury accessory consists of white gold bracelet frosted in 260 carats of clear white diamonds. It also has a skeleton dial. The item may be considered a man’s version of the Fascination by Graff Diamonds.

8. Paul Newman Rolex Daytona, $17.8 million

$17.8 million is a fair price for the luxury item worn by Paul Newman himself. The watch was made in 1968 and was presented to the American actor by his beloved wife. Later, the item was sold at an auction at the aforementioned price.

9. Patek Philippe Ref. 1518 in Stainless Steel, $11.1 million

The beautiful and simple watch was created back in 1941. The item is remarkable because, at those times, only this watch had a perpetual calendar. In 2016, it was bought at an auction for $11.1 million.

10. Vacheron Constantin 57260, $8 million

With 57 complications and 2,800 parts, the Vacheron Constantin deserves a title of a truly unique accessory. It is totally handmade, which makes the watch even more impressive. It certainly deserves its price.
